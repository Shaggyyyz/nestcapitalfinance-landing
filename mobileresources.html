Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about home loans, applications, and our services.

What documents do I need to apply for a home loan?

Typically, you'll need proof of identity (driver's license or passport), proof of income (payslips or tax returns), bank statements for the last 3-6 months, details of assets and liabilities, and information about the property you're purchasing. We'll guide you through the exact requirements based on your situation.

How much deposit do I need to buy a home?

While a 20% deposit is ideal to avoid Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI), many lenders accept deposits as low as 5-10%. First home buyers may also access government schemes that allow lower deposits. We can help you explore all available options.

What is Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI)?

LMI is insurance that protects the lender if you default on your loan. It's typically required when your deposit is less than 20% of the property value. The cost varies based on your loan amount and deposit size, and it's usually added to your loan or paid upfront.

How long does the home loan approval process take?

Pre-approval can take 1-3 business days, while full approval typically takes 5-10 business days once all documentation is submitted. Complex applications or additional property valuations may extend this timeframe. We work to expedite the process wherever possible.

Can I get a home loan if I'm self-employed?

Absolutely! Self-employed borrowers can access home loans, though you'll typically need to provide 2 years of tax returns and financial statements. Some lenders offer low-doc loans with alternative income verification methods. We specialize in finding the right solutions for self-employed clients.

What's the difference between pre-approval and approval?

Pre-approval (or conditional approval) is an indication of how much you can borrow based on your financial situation. Full approval is granted once the lender verifies all documentation and values the property. Pre-approval gives you confidence when making offers, but it's subject to final conditions.

Should I fix or keep my interest rate variable?

This depends on your risk tolerance, market conditions, and financial goals. Fixed rates offer payment certainty, while variable rates provide flexibility and potential savings if rates drop. Many clients choose a split loan combining both. We'll help you assess what's best for your circumstances.

Do you charge fees for your service?

For most home loan applications, our service is completely free—we're paid a commission by the lender when your loan settles. For complex scenarios or specialized services, we'll discuss any fees upfront. Transparency is at the core of everything we do.
